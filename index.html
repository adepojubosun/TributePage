Elon Musk's Life and Achievements

  • June 28, 1971 - Born Elon Reeve Musk, in Pretoria, Transvaal, South Africa.
  • 1980 - Moves in with father after parents divorce.
  • 1981 - Develops interest in computing with the Commodore VIC-20.
  • 1983 - Teaches himself computer programming at just 12 years old. Developed BASIC-based video game called Blastar and sells it to a magazine called PC and Office Technology for $500.
  • 1989 - Graduates from Pretoria Boys High School and moves to Canada after obtaining Canadian Citizenship through his Canadian Born Mother.
  • 1990 - Acccepted into Queen's University in Kingston, Ontario for undergraduate study.
  • 1992 - Transfers to the University of Pennsylvania.
  • 1995 - Starts Zip2, a web software company with brother Kimbal.
  • 1997 - Recieves a Bachelor of Science degree in Physics from the University of Pennsylvania and Bachelor of Science in Economics from Warton School of Business.
  • 1999 - Compaq acquires Zip2 for $307 million in cash and $34 million in stock options.
  • 1999 - Cofounds X.com, an online financial services and email payment company.
  • 2000 - X.com merges with Confinity, a company who had a money transfer service called Paypal.
  • 2002 - Paypal acquired by ebay for $1.5 billion in stocks, of which Elon Musk recieves $165 million.
  • 2002 - Becomes a U.S. Citizen.
  • 2002 - Founds Space Exploration Technologies(SpaceX), a company that focuses on space exploration and manufacturing of space launch vehicles.
  • 2004 - Joins the board electric car manufacturing startup company Tesla Motors.
  • 2006 - Cofounds SolarCity with Lyndon and Peter Rive, a solar power company.
  • 2006 - SpaceX awarded a contract from NASA to continue development and test of the falcon 9 launch vehicles and also the Dragon spacecraft for cargo transport to the International Space Station.
  • 2007 - Becomes CEO and product architect of Tesla Motors.
  • 2010 - Ranked as the No. 10 (tied with rocketry pioneer and scientist Wernher von Braun) most popular space hero.
  • 2011 - Listed on forbes as one of "America's 20 Most Powerful CEOs 40 And Under"
  • 2011 - Awarded the US$250,000 Heinlein Prize for Advances in Space Commercialization.
  • 2012 - Awarded the Royal Aeronautical Society's highest award: a Gold Medal.
  • 2013 - Musk was named the Fortune Businessperson of the year for SpaceX, SolarCity, and Tesla.
  • 2015 - Awarded IEEE Honorary Membership.
  • 2016 - Named by Business Insider one of the "Top 10 Business Visionaries Creating Value for the World" along with Mark Zuckerberg and Sal Khan.
  • 2016 - Ranked 21st on Forbes list of The World's Most Powerful People.
  • 2017 - Listed by UK-based company Richtopia at number 3 in the list of 200 Most Influential Philanthropists and Social Entrepreneurs.
  • 2017 - Elon Musk's SpaceX becomes largest private producer of rocket engines in the world.
